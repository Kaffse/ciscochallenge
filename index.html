The Internet of Everything Challenge

It's time to explore the future

The time has come to join together as teams to discover the potential of the Internet of Everything.

Your task is simple. You must formulate an idea, refine it and complile that idea to a short video.

The video can be up to 5 minutes long and can be hosted on Youtube or any similar service.

Your video must showcase your idea, and will be required for your team to have a chance to win.

However, implementing your idea and submitting working code will certainly get you plenty of bonus marks towards the wonderful hardware prizes provided by Cisco!

What's this about?

Cisco systems have kindly made avaliable 18 Smartcitizen kits to the University of Glasgow. These outstanding little devices can record all kinds of interesting data: light, sound, CO2, NO...

With these awesome devices the University of Glasgow is running a challenge during June and July 2014, in partnership with Cisco, allowing you to show the world just what the future could look like with an "Internet of Everything".

With the opening event on the 28th May 2014 as a keynote and chance for teams to form and ideas to flow, the challenge will begin. Each team is given around 8 weeks to formulate, then show off their idea.

All teams will have an opportunity to have their entry showcased at the Glasgow Science Centre during the Glasgow Commonwealth Games. Winners will be chosen and presented with Cisco hardware.
